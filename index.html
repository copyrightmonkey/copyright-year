CopyrightYear

Stop hard-coding your copyright dates. It's , this shouldn't be hard.

Why This Matters

An outdated copyright date is a quality signal. When your site says "© " in , you're broadcasting:

  • Nobody's maintaining this
  • Attention to detail is lacking
  • The site might be abandoned

If you can't update a four-digit number once a year, what else are you neglecting?

The Solutions

Pick your poison. All of these work. No frameworks required.

Plain HTML + JavaScript

Client-side (works anywhere)
<p>Copyright © 1992-<span id="year"></span> Your Company</p> <script> document.getElementById('year').textContent = new Date().getFullYear(); </script>
Output: Copyright © 1992- Your Company

PHP (WordPress, Laravel, etc.)

Server-side rendering
Copyright © 1992-<?php echo date('Y'); ?> Your Company

Python (Flask, Django)

Template context
from datetime import datetime # In your view/context: context = { 'current_year': datetime.now().year } # In template: Copyright © 1992-{{ current_year }} Your Company

Ruby (Rails, Jekyll)

ERB/Liquid templates
Copyright © 1992-<%= Time.now.year %> Your Company # Or in Jekyll (_config.yml): start_year: 1992 # Then in template: Copyright © {{ site.start_year }}-{{ 'now' | date: "%Y" }} Your Company

Node.js (Express, Next.js)

Template engines
// In your view context: res.render('page', { currentYear: new Date().getFullYear() }); // In template: Copyright © 1992-{{ currentYear }} Your Company // Or React component: <footer> Copyright © 1992-{new Date().getFullYear()} Your Company </footer>

Hugo (Static Site Generator)

Built-in template function
Copyright © 1992-{{ now.Year }} Your Company

Eleventy (11ty)

Nunjucks/Liquid shortcode
// .eleventy.js module.exports = function(eleventyConfig) { eleventyConfig.addShortcode("year", () => `${new Date().getFullYear()}`); }; // In template: Copyright © 1992-{% year %} Your Company

Semantic HTML Bonus

Use the <time> element for machine-readable dates:

Accessible & semantic
<footer> <p>Copyright © <time datetime="1992">1992</time>-<time id="current-year" datetime=""></time> Your Company </p> </footer> <script> const year = new Date().getFullYear(); const el = document.getElementById('current-year'); el.textContent = year; el.setAttribute('datetime', year); </script>

The Hall of Shame

Things you should not do:

❌ Copyright © 2019 YourCompany (hard-coded, forgotten) ❌ Copyright © 2019-2021 YourCompany (abandoned mid-pandemic) ❌ Copyright © 2026 YourCompany (we did this initially, fixed it before launch) ❌ Copyright © <!-- TODO: update this --> (developer honesty, but still broken)
